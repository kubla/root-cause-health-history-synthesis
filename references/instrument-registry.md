# Instrument Registry

Use instruments as targeted probes, not as mandatory rituals.

For every instrument:

- explain why it is being used
- summarize the result cleanly
- map the result back into the case model
- avoid letting the instrument replace narrative understanding

## ACE Questionnaire

- Domain: adversity and early-life burden
- Trigger criteria: early-life adversity may shape vulnerability, stress physiology, coping, or symptom persistence
- Expected value: contextualizes vulnerability and long-range stress loading
- Administration notes: ask permission before using it
- Scoring notes: record total score and whether specific categories were endorsed if the user volunteers them
- Summary rule: treat as contextual vulnerability, not a deterministic explanation
- Snapshot representation: include score, date, and why it matters

## Broad symptom inventory

- Domain: baseline symptom landscape
- Trigger criteria: the case is diffuse and needs initial compression
- Expected value: helps identify clusters and triage where to go deeper
- Administration notes: use as a scan, then return to narrative and cluster-building
- Scoring notes: do not over-interpret totals
- Summary rule: convert items into symptom clusters with timing and impact
- Snapshot representation: summarize dominant clusters rather than raw checklist outputs

## PHQ-9

- Domain: depressive symptoms
- Trigger criteria: mood, anhedonia, motivation, or depressive burden may affect the case
- Expected value: helps distinguish burden level and supports structured handoff
- Administration notes: if suicidality is endorsed, break normal flow and address safety
- Scoring notes: report total and rough severity band
- Summary rule: note whether symptoms seem primary, reactive, or mixed
- Snapshot representation: include score, date, context, and relevant cautions

## GAD-7

- Domain: anxiety symptoms
- Trigger criteria: anxiety may be a major cluster, mediator, or perpetuator
- Expected value: clarifies symptom burden and can improve clinician handoff
- Administration notes: use when anxiety is prominent or ambiguous
- Scoring notes: report total and rough severity band
- Summary rule: connect anxiety pattern to timeline, sleep, stress, and bodily symptoms
- Snapshot representation: include score, date, and links to relevant clusters

## STOP-Bang

- Domain: sleep-disordered breathing risk
- Trigger criteria: fatigue, snoring, witnessed apneas, hypertension, or nonrestorative sleep
- Expected value: flags whether sleep apnea should be more central to the case
- Administration notes: use as a risk screen, not a diagnosis
- Scoring notes: report total and risk band
- Summary rule: treat as a reason to elevate sleep-disordered breathing as a live question
- Snapshot representation: include score, risk interpretation, and next-step implication

## Epworth Sleepiness Scale

- Domain: daytime sleepiness
- Trigger criteria: hypersomnolence, napping, or unclear daytime sleep burden
- Expected value: sharpens the sleep cluster and specialist handoff
- Administration notes: use when daytime sleepiness is clinically relevant
- Scoring notes: report total and whether sleepiness seems disproportionate
- Summary rule: pair with sleep quantity, quality, and circadian pattern
- Snapshot representation: include score, date, and practical interpretation

## Instrument handling rules

- Use instruments only when they materially improve the case.
- Do not stack instruments just because they are available.
- When an instrument result conflicts with the narrative, preserve the tension instead of forcing agreement.
- Prefer concise summaries over raw item dumps.
